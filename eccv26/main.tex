\caption{Overview of our motion-aware token sparsification framework. Given a template and a search region, we insert a sparsification module into the Transformer encoder to retain the top-$K$ search tokens for subsequent computation. The token selection is guided by attention-based relevance together with a temporal motion prior from the previous prediction, enabling early pruning while preserving target-relevant information. The resulting sparse tokens are then decoded by a lightweight MLP-based sparse head that operates directly on the unstructured token set with a score-first, regress-once pipeline, avoiding dense reshaping and reducing redundant prediction computation.}

\section{Conclusion}
In this work, we demonstrated the effectiveness of motion-aware token sparsification for efficient visual tracking, making it suitable for real-time applications. However, one limitation of our approach lies in the reduced receptive field compared to methods that perform dense computation over the entire local search region. We hope our experiments inspire more research on temporal motion priors for lightweight and accurate tracking.
